## 1 Introduction


### 1.1 Purpose of the device

The ZonEcrón©, and when we say ZonEcrón© we want you to imagine neon lights and fireworks in the background (okay, I’ll skip this part for the rest of the manual, or it will get too long), as I was saying, the ZonEcrón© was designed to meet the need of timing execution in zones (gateway, fence, and seesaw) and logically, it would also allow you to time short sequences to determine which option is best.

Under this premise, given our nature of minimal effort, we added a series of requirements, such as easy installation, wireless, etc., and began experimenting with different technologies (infrared, radio, batteries, screens, etc.) that brought us closer to our final result. 
Ultimately, ZonEcrón© was born with the aim of meeting the timing needs in agility training, and this is the environment we intend to limit it to.

Although the device’s features allow it to be used as a competition timer, some additions (larger screen, connection to trial software, an extra pair of spares, etc.) would be recommended to use it for this purpose.


### 1.2 Main features

- Compact, small size with all necessary features integrated.
- Quick to set up, not tedious to assemble, or at least not too much.
- Suitable for outdoor use, visible in the sun, and with some protection against rain.
- Rechargeable, via USB, with over 30 hours of autonomy.
- Safe: Harmless signals (infrared and wifi) and no sharp corners.
- Firm attachment on the wing for any material (plastic, aluminum, iron).
- Wireless. No cables are used except for charging the batteries.
- Long communication range. Tested up to 80m, recommended 40m.
- Integrated 4-digit display with the highest resolution allowed by the time to be displayed (milliseconds, hundredths, tenths, or seconds).
- Dual sensor, upper and lower, on each gate.

## 2 Usage


### 2.1 Power on and placement

A pair of ZonEcrón© originals consists of the infrared emitter (the box without a screen) and the infrared receiver (the box with a screen).

Both devices have a clamp with one side fixed to the box and the other sliding, guided by two steel rods. Using an internal rubber band, both sides of the clamp stay together. This rubber can be quickly adjusted to 3 tension levels by moving the knotted ends of the rubber band, allowing it to fit different thicknesses of the wing or post structure. See photos below:

Sliding the clamp to open it, we place the ZonEcrón© on the vertical part of the wing (or on a post of the long jump). The emitter should go on the right and the receiver on the left, aligning the two emitters with the two receivers roughly as shown:
![Double Sensor](../images/original/doubleSensor.png)

Next, we proceed to turn on the emitter and receiver. 
To verify that the emitter is on, simply check the blue LED, which will be solid or blinking (if the battery is low). 
For the receiver, the blue light will turn on immediately when powered, and several messages will appear on the display. Once the time shows zero (0.0) on the screen, the blue light will turn off if the receiver correctly detects the emitter's infrared signal. Although the power-on and setup sequence is not critical, we recommend first placing the devices in position and then powering on the emitter (without screen), so that when powering on the receiver (with screen), you can check the battery levels of both devices in the initial screen messages.

**It is crucial** to leave space between the post and the lower emitter to maximize detection chances. We recommend that the lower emitter be positioned 10 cm above the post for XS, S, and M sizes, and 15 cm above the post for L and XL sizes.

### 2.2 Alignment and detection

Once the emitter and receiver are positioned facing each other and turned on, the blue light on the receiver will turn off if the alignment is correct and there are no obstacles interrupting the infrared beam. When the beam is interrupted (by a pass or misalignment), the blue LED on the receiver will turn on for at least 0.5 seconds, or if the interruption lasts longer, it will stay on for that duration.

The emitter has 2 infrared LEDs, one upper and one lower, and the receiver has an upper and a lower receptor. The reception is selective, meaning the upper receptor only responds to the infrared signal sent by the upper emitter. The same applies to the lower pair. This creates two straight barriers from the emitter to the receiver. There is no cross detection.  
If either of these barriers is interrupted, the timing will start or stop. Be cautious when placing the emitter and receiver too close to each other or parallel to walls, as the emitter's power is strong enough to bounce off nearby solid objects, or its (invisible) glow can bypass an object, such as a hand, if both are placed on a table for testing purposes, for example.

We recommend a minimum operational distance of 1 meter. The maximum distance depends on the amount of ambient light. It ranges from 2 meters in full daylight to up to 20 meters in darkness with artificial spot lighting.


### 2.3 Infrared auto-interference

Due to the power used by the infrared signals to function in full sunlight, it may occur that a receiver picks up infrared signals from 2 emitters. This effect is more pronounced in low-light conditions or enclosed spaces.  
The most obvious symptom is when the blue light on a receiver keeps turning on and off, and the timing may start and stop without the beam being interrupted.

This happens when a receiver receives infrared signals from two emitters simultaneously, either due to the positioning on the course or by bouncing off nearby surfaces (walls or glass). For example, in this case, receiver 1 (R1) receives infrared signals from emitter 1 (E1) and emitter 2 (E2):

### 2.4 Radio communication

Radio communication is automatically established between all ZonEcrón© devices. This communication works in the wifi frequency range and may be affected if used in environments with many wifi networks present.

The ZonEcrón© original has an internal antenna in each receiver (the box with the screen) to establish this communication. Optimal communication reliability and range are achieved when both receivers are in a vertical position, i.e., their usual mounting position.

The theoretical maximum range is 80 meters in open field. Experimentally, a range of 200 meters has been tested in a park within a residential area with multiple surrounding wifi networks, with no communication failures. It is recommended not to exceed 40 meters in use on a regulation-sized course for optimal performance.


### 2.5 Display

Once the receiver is turned on and the various messages have been displayed as explained earlier, the display will show “0.0” in the center digits. If the timing starts on either of the two emitter-receiver pairs, the time will begin to run on both screens. While the time is running, the seconds and tenths of a second will be displayed, and the position of the digits may shift if the seconds exceed 99, or the tenths will disappear if the seconds exceed 999.

Once the timing is stopped, the display will show the time with the highest possible resolution:

| Resolution  | From   | To     | Example |
|-------------|--------|--------|---------|
| Milliseconds| 0.000s | 9.999s | 5.417   |
| Hundredths  | 10.00s | 99.99s | 54.17   |
| Tenths      | 100.0s | 999.9s | 541.7   |
| Seconds     | 1000s  | 9999s  | 5417.   |

Both screens will display the same time, as they synchronize at the start, during, and at the end of the timing.


### 2.6 Power and Recharging

All four devices, two emitters and two receivers, have a built-in rechargeable lithium battery. Therefore, no power or communication cables are needed during use.

Battery levels can be checked by turning on a receiver while its emitter is on. Additionally, the emitters (without a screen) will indicate a low battery level by slowly flashing the blue LED and a very low battery level by flashing it rapidly.

This battery level indication **is approximate**, as what is actually measured is the battery voltage, which is more or less related to the remaining charge but is not foolproof. Therefore, you might notice that the percentage drops quickly from 100% to 90%, then stays fairly steady between 90% and 10%, and then drops quickly from 10% to 0%. It may also happen that the indicated percentage fluctuates during successive on and off cycles, so we insist that this is an approximate indication.

The devices have a USB-C port on the bottom. To recharge the batteries, they must be off, and you just need to connect a standard USB cable to a USB charger or a computer's USB port. Due to the different connectors on USB cables, it is physically impossible to connect them incorrectly, so even the most clumsy are safe.

**CAUTION**: In previous versions, the connector on the device was micro-USB, and it is possible to damage this connector if you insist on inserting the charging cable head in the wrong position, as it only accepts one orientation.

**WARNING: DO NOT CHARGE THE DEVICES WITHOUT SUPERVISION.**  
No battery-powered device should be charged without supervision. It's common to leave a phone plugged in overnight, and nothing happens. But the fact that nothing happens doesn't mean that something couldn't. Not long ago, a large company had problems with their phone batteries overheating when charging, eventually causing them to explode. Therefore, protect yourself and those around you. It's not necessary to stare at the device for four hours to make sure everything goes well, but if you are charging the devices, it's recommended to stay nearby doing other tasks. If you cannot stay close, it is advisable to disconnect them and continue charging at another time. These batteries do not have memory, and they can be charged in stages without any problem.

**WARNING: CHECK AFTER IMPACT.**  
It is highly likely that at some point, a dog will impact the wing where the ZonEcrón© is mounted or directly impact the ZonEcrón© itself. The design of the ZonEcrón© has focused on making the device as resistant as possible and securely holding the internal parts, but in such circumstances, it is mandatory to inspect the device thoroughly. If any damage is observed, if loose parts are heard inside, or if it overheats while charging, the device should be turned off and disconnected immediately and placed in a location where it cannot cause a fire. Contact us to discuss possible solutions.

That said, the ZonEcrón© features an electronic circuit that controls the charging and discharging of the batteries. It prevents them from being overcharged or overdischarged. The 0% battery indication, or the fast blinking of the emitter’s LED, is the minimum recommended battery level, at which point the ZonEcrón© should be recharged. However, it will remain on, draining the battery to the maximum until the protection circuit cuts off the power. This allows the ZonEcrón© to be used in emergency situations, but it is not recommended as regular practice, as it will negatively affect the battery's lifespan.

Lastly, the popular wisdom recommending fully draining the battery before recharging it applies to old Ni-Cd batteries. For these "new" (this technology is no longer new) lithium batteries, it is better not to fully discharge them (in fact, it is harmful). It is much more advisable to recharge them when they are at half charge. Even charging them in intervals, for a while now and then, has no negative impact.


### 2.7 Autonomy

A new original ZonEcrón has an autonomy of over 40 hours, a very wide margin for use as cells in a competition timer for a one-day event and more than enough for training. This autonomy will decrease over time due to the normal lifespan of lithium batteries.

Ambient temperature can also negatively affect battery life:
- For usage, the colder it is, the shorter the duration. It is not advisable to use the devices in temperatures below 0ºC.
- For charging, it is recommended to do so at moderate temperatures between 10ºC and 30ºC to ensure proper and complete charging.

To increase autonomy, the following strategies have been adopted:
- When the timer is running, the screen brightness is reduced since the final time measurement is the only useful information. When the timer is stopped, the screen brightness is set to maximum.
- Once the timer is stopped, after 30 seconds of inactivity, the screen brightness is reduced again. After another 30 seconds, the screen will turn off and only blink for 1 second every 5 seconds.


## 2.8 Sun and Rain

The original ZonEcrón has been designed to function perfectly outdoors in the sun or rain.
- Its characteristic white color has been intentionally chosen to prevent it from overheating under the sun.
- Its design allows for use in the rain as long as it is positioned vertically with the connectors facing down to prevent water from entering through those openings.


## 2.9 Storage

When storing the ZonEcrón, consider both humidity and the battery:
- First, and as already mentioned, the ZonEcrón is waterproof but not humidity-proof. If kept in a humid environment for several days, moisture will slowly penetrate the casing and may irreparably damage the electronic components. Therefore, if it has been used in the rain, it should be left in a dry environment for a day before storing it to completely eliminate any potential moisture.
- Second, regarding the battery, if you plan not to use the ZonEcrón© for an extended period, it is best to leave the batteries at half charge to maximize their lifespan. Storing lithium batteries fully charged or fully discharged for prolonged periods can significantly decrease their performance.
